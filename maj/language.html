Maj Description

The most valuable resource of humanity is its capacity for communication and reasoning. But what if the languages we use today are a bottleneck? Maj is a new language designed from the ground up to be more efficient, logical, and expressive, acting as a direct upgrade to our thought processes.

Maj language is logical, engineered language, designed to be simple and regular. It has about 5200 words. This is not a programming language, is a communication solution designed for, reasoning, chat and games. Because is simple anyone can learn it faster than normal human spoken languages that are much more difficult.

I am a polymath who has taught himself eight languages and fixed millions of lines of code. I have a deep understanding of what makes a language easy or hard to learn. I've taken all of that knowledge and built a perfect language from the ground up, designed to be an ideal tool for human communication and learning. This isn't just another conlang, this is a solution to a problem, humans do not even acknowledge that exists. And this problem is a big! Otherwise all humans would be able to learn a second language.

Maj Reasons

There are some good reasons to make a new language:

  • Erosion of linguistic diversity has reduced the number of languages to around 6,500;
  • Languages become more complex as time pass and therefore difficult to learn;
  • Languages are difficult to optimize due to government intervention and static culture;
  • Languages have errors that nobody care to fix that makes some of them ambiguous;
  • Languages progress is real slow, lagging behind scientific and technologic descoveries.

Maj Purpose:

Maj language has several potential use-cases:

  1. playground for creative software in language processing and games;
  2. learning how to use tools to organize a large and challenging project;
  3. study grammar and vocabulary of other languages like Romanian, French, Spanish, Portugese and Italian;
  4. experiment with different words and sounds comming from other languages;

Maj Attributes:

Maj language, will have the following attributes:

  • Regular - with deterministic reading rules;
  • Efficient - short words and simple grammar;
  • Innovative - logic and comprehensive;

Maj is Regular

Maj have shorter words to reduce cost of typing; so Maj can be faster and more efficient than other languages; after initial calculation we estimate Maj is on average 20% more efficient than other languages. Also we try to make Maj more efficient by reducing number of words required in sentences; therefore Maj has an unusually large number of verbs.

Rules:

  • Maj frequent words are shorter while less frequent words are longer;
  • Maj can use prefixes and suffixes to make a new word from a root;
  • Maj letters are pronounced the same in all words regardless of word origin;

Maj is Efficient

We strive to make Maj efficient. Our main method is to make short words. We aim for Maj to be crisp and elegant easy to pronounce. We avoid long clasters of consonants (3 or more consonants) but we use sometimes two consonants side by side, and make words shorter.

Many words are related in Maj by using a common root with different ending vowel: {"a", "e", "o", "u", "h", "i"} to create groups of related words. In the next example you can see a regular noun and a regular verb.

Regular Noun:

  • poma = fruit
  • pome = fruits
  • pomo = fruitful

Verb example:

  • doza = dizziness
  • dozo = dizzy
  • dozu = getting dizzy

This verb can change the status of something or somebody. The new status becomes an adjective. Also this verb ce infer a new abstract thing called "doza". And this can be used as a noun. Maj is rigid, do not use other forms. If we need another form for a root we may add more letters

Maj is Experimental:

Maj is experimental and not perfect. In our defense it was created simple on purpose to be logic and easy to learn. One major issue of Maj is the high similarity between two short words. Sometimes words that have nothing in common may look alike, having just a vowel difference. We try to identify these situations by creating longer words.

Examples:

  • rota = wheel
  • ruta = route

One single letter can be read wrong and this can change the meaning of the sentence. Maj is not error tolerant and this is bad because we can not use error correction algorithms. There will be no spell check for Maj. You must be careful to type it correctly.
